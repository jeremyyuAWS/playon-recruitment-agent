## State-Specific Requirements

### Georgia (HQ)
- New hire reporting: within 10 days
- No salary history ban (as of current law)
- No pay transparency posting requirement (as of current law)
- At-will employment state

### States with Pay Transparency Laws (if hiring remotely)
| State | Requirement | Trigger |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Colorado | Salary range in posting | All CO-accessible postings |
| California | Salary range in posting + to current employees on request | All CA-accessible postings |
| New York | Salary range in posting (NYC + statewide) | All NY-accessible postings |
| Washington | Salary range in posting | All WA-accessible postings |
| Connecticut | Salary range on request or by offer stage | Provide upon request |
| Maryland | Salary range on request | Provide upon request |

### Ban-the-Box (Criminal History)
| Jurisdiction | Rule |
|-------------|------|
| Federal contractors | Cannot ask until conditional offer |
| CA, NY, NJ, MA, IL, etc. | Cannot ask on application; timing varies |
| Georgia | No statewide ban (check local ordinances) |

## Immigration Compliance

| Task | Deadline | Tool |
|------|----------|------|
| I-9 completion | Within 3 business days of start | Manual + HRIS |
| I-9 reverification | Before work authorization expires | immigration-tracker hook |
| H-1B LCA posting | 30 days at worksite | Manual |
| E-Verify | Within 3 business days of hire (if enrolled) | E-Verify portal |
| PERM audit file retention | 5 years from filing | compliance/ directory |

## Data Retention Requirements

| Data Type | Retention Period | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Applications (not hired) | 2 years (OFCCP: 2 years) | Lever retention policy |
| I-9 forms | 3 years from hire OR 1 year from termination (whichever later) | Secure storage |
| Payroll records | 3 years (FLSA) | Payroll system |
| Benefits records | 6 years (ERISA) | Benefits system |
| EEO-1 reports | 3 years minimum | compliance/ directory |
| Interview notes | 2 years | Lever |

*Review this calendar quarterly. Employment law changes frequently — verify with legal counsel annually.*
