# Rejection Communication

## Instructions

Send timely, empathetic, stage-appropriate rejections that protect PlayOn's employer brand and leave the door open for future opportunities.

### Principles
- **Speed**: Reject within 48 hours of decision — silence is the worst rejection
- **Personalization scales with depth**: The further a candidate progressed, the more personal the rejection
- **Honesty without liability**: Be genuine but avoid language that creates legal exposure
- **Future-facing**: Every rejection should feel like "not now" rather than "never"

### Stage-Specific Approaches

#### Stage 1: Application Screen (Automated)
- **Timing**: Within 1 week of application
- **Channel**: Email (automated)
- **Personalization**: Name + role title only
- **Feedback**: None (volume makes individual feedback impossible at 2,500 applications)
- **CRM**: Do not add (too early in process)

#### Stage 2: Pre-Screen Rejection
- **Timing**: Within 48 hours of pre-screen completion
- **Channel**: Email (templated with personalization)
- **Personalization**: Name + role + 1 specific observation from pre-screen
- **Feedback**: Brief reason category ("we're prioritizing candidates with X experience")
- **CRM**: Add if scored 60+ on initial screen

#### Stage 3: Post-Interview Rejection
- **Timing**: Within 48 hours of hiring decision
- **Channel**: Phone call from recruiter (preferred) + follow-up email
- **Personalization**: Reference specific interview conversations, acknowledge time invested
- **Feedback**: 2-3 sentences of constructive, actionable feedback
- **CRM**: Always add — these are warm candidates

#### Stage 4: Finalist / Runner-Up Rejection
- **Timing**: Same day as offer acceptance by selected candidate
- **Channel**: Phone call from recruiter (required) + personal email
- **Personalization**: Highly personal — reference specific strengths, express genuine regret
- **Feedback**: Detailed feedback if candidate requests it (schedule a call)
- **CRM**: Priority add — flag as "silver medal" for future roles
- **Special**: Recruiter should offer to keep in touch and proactively reach out for future roles

### Message Templates

#### Screen Rejection
```
Subject: Your application to PlayOn Sports — [Role Title]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for your interest in the [Role Title] position at PlayOn Sports.
After reviewing your application, we've decided to move forward with
candidates whose experience more closely aligns with our current needs.

We appreciate the time you took to apply and encourage you to check our
careers page for future opportunities that may be a better fit.

Best,
PlayOn Sports Recruiting Team
```

#### Post-Interview Rejection
```
Subject: Update on your [Role Title] candidacy at PlayOn Sports

Hi [Name],

Thank you for taking the time to interview with our team for the [Role Title]
position. We enjoyed learning about your experience with [specific topic
discussed].

After careful consideration, we've decided to move forward with another
candidate. This was a competitive process, and your [specific strength]
stood out to our team.

[1-2 sentences of constructive feedback if appropriate]

We'd love to stay in touch — [specific reason to reconnect, e.g., "we
anticipate similar roles opening in Q3"]. Would you be open to that?

Warm regards,
[Recruiter Name]
```

### What NOT to Say
- Never: "You weren't qualified" → Instead: "We prioritized candidates with [specific skill]"
- Never: "You were our second choice" → Instead: "This was an extremely competitive process"
- Never: specific comparison to the selected candidate
- Never: "Maybe next time" (dismissive) → Instead: "We'd genuinely like to stay connected"
- Never: anything about protected characteristics, even positively
- Never: promise a future role or guarantee re-engagement

### Metrics
- **Rejection timeliness**: % sent within 48 hours of decision (target: 95%)
- **Candidate NPS post-rejection**: Survey 1 week after rejection (target: 30+)
- **CRM opt-in rate**: % of rejected candidates who agree to future outreach (target: 40%)
- **Re-application rate**: % of rejected candidates who apply again within 12 months

### Rules
- All rejection templates must be approved by HR and legal before first use
- Phone rejections for Stage 3+ are mandatory, not optional
- Never reject on a Friday afternoon — schedule for Monday-Thursday, morning preferred
- If a candidate asks "why not me?", offer a scheduled feedback call within 1 week
- Silver medal candidates get priority sourcing outreach for the next matching role
