# Hiring Manager Intake

## Instructions

Conduct a structured intake session with the hiring manager before any recruiting activity begins. This is the foundation for everything — a bad intake produces bad candidates.

### Intake Agenda (45-60 minutes)

#### 1. Role Context (10 min)
- Why is this role open? (new headcount, backfill, restructure)
- What problem does this hire solve?
- What happens if we don't fill this in 30/60/90 days?
- Who did this job before? What worked and what didn't?

#### 2. Must-Haves vs. Nice-to-Haves (15 min)
- "If you could only have 3 requirements, what are they?"
- For each requirement: "What does 'good enough' look like vs. 'exceptional'?"
- "What's a skill you've listed in past JDs that turned out not to matter?"
- "What skill did you NOT list that your best hire had?"
- Years of experience: "Is this actually years, or is it a proxy for [specific capability]?"

#### 3. Hidden Preferences (10 min)
These are the unwritten criteria that drive most rejections:
- "Describe your last 3 hires who succeeded. What did they have in common?"
- "Describe a hire who looked great on paper but didn't work out. Why?"
- "What's the team's working style? Who would NOT fit?"
- "What does a typical day/week look like in this role?"
- Company/industry experience: "Does it actually matter, or is domain knowledge learnable?"

#### 4. Team Dynamics (5 min)
- Team size and structure
- Who will this person work with most?
- Management style: how much autonomy vs. direction?
- Remote/hybrid expectations for this specific role

#### 5. Interview Process (5 min)
- Suggested interview panel (who evaluates what)
- Technical assessment: needed? What type?
- Timeline expectations: when do you need someone started?
- Decision-making: who has final say?

#### 6. Compensation & Selling Points (5 min)
- Approved compensation range (confirm with finance)
- What makes this role compelling? (growth, impact, tech, team)
- Any flexibility on level? (could you hire senior if no staff available?)
- Relocation/remote flexibility

### Output: Intake Brief

```
## Hiring Manager Intake Brief
Date: [Date]
Hiring Manager: [Name]
Role: [Title] — [Department]
Recruiter: [Name]

### The Why
- Open because: [reason]
- Impact if unfilled: [consequence]
- Priority: [Critical / High / Standard]

### Requirements
Must-Haves (non-negotiable):
1. [Requirement] — evidence: [how to verify]
2. [Requirement] — evidence: [how to verify]
3. [Requirement] — evidence: [how to verify]

Nice-to-Haves (differentiate top candidates):
1. [Requirement]
2. [Requirement]

Explicitly NOT Required:
1. [Thing that might appear in JD but HM confirmed doesn't matter]

### Hidden Criteria
- Success profile: [description of ideal candidate beyond resume]
- Failure pattern: [what didn't work in past hires]
- Team culture: [working style, autonomy level, collaboration pattern]

### Process
- Interview panel: [Names + what each evaluates]
- Assessment: [Type or N/A]
- Timeline: [Target start date]
- Decision maker: [Name]

### Comp & Sell
- Range: $[X] - $[Y] base + [bonus/equity]
- Top selling points: [3 bullet points for sourcing outreach]
- Flexibility: [Level flex, remote flex, etc.]

### Recruiter Notes
- [Any observations, concerns, or follow-ups needed]
```

### After Intake
1. Share brief with hiring manager for sign-off (24h turnaround)
2. Feed must-haves into stack-ranking skill scoring weights
3. Feed selling points into candidate-sourcing outreach
4. Feed interview panel into calendar-scheduling skill
5. Route to job-description-optimizer skill to draft JD

### Rules
- Never skip the intake — even for "urgent" roles. A 45-minute intake saves weeks of mis-hires
- If the hiring manager can't articulate 3 must-haves, push back — vague requirements produce vague candidates
- Document hidden preferences explicitly — they're the #1 source of late-stage rejections
- If stated YOE requirement seems inflated, challenge it respectfully with market data
- Intake brief is a living document — update when requirements shift mid-search
